## 🛡️ Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

A practical guide for college students in India to stay safe
from cybercrimes including UPI fraud, phishing, identity theft,
cyberbullying, and ransomware.

---

### ✅ General Online Safety Tips

| No. | Prevention Tip | Why It Matters |
|-----|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | Always use strong, unique passwords for every online account. Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. | Weak passwords are the #1 reason accounts get hacked |
| 2 | Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. | Even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without the OTP |
| 3 | Never click on unknown or suspicious links received via WhatsApp, SMS, email, or social media — even if they appear to come from friends. | Phishing links are designed to look real and steal your login details |
| 4 | Always verify the identity of anyone who contacts you online claiming to offer scholarships, prizes, jobs, or government benefits. | Most such offers are scams designed to steal money or personal data |
| 5 | Do not share personal information — Aadhaar number, PAN card, date of birth, home address — on unverified websites or with strangers online. | Personal data is used for identity theft and financial fraud |
| 6 | Regularly update your phone, apps, and antivirus software to the latest version. | Updates fix security vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit |

---

### 💰 UPI and Financial Safety Tips (Mandatory)

| No. | UPI / Financial Safety Tip | Why It Matters |
|-----|---------------------------|----------------|
| 7 | NEVER enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money. You only need your PIN to SEND money — if someone asks for your PIN to transfer funds to you, it is 100% a fraud. | This is the most common UPI scam trap used to trick students |
| 8 | Always check the UPI request carefully before approving — a "collect request" means money is going OUT of your account, not coming IN. | Fraudsters send collect requests disguised as payment receipts |
| 9 | Never share your OTP, CVV, card number, or net banking password with anyone — including people claiming to be bank officials or customer care representatives. | Legitimate bank officials will NEVER ask for your OTP or PIN |
| 10 | Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your bank app to minimize loss in case of fraud. | Limits the maximum amount that can be stolen in a single incident |

---

### 📱 Social Media and Identity Safety Tips

| No. | Safety Tip | Why It Matters |
|-----|-----------|----------------|
| 11 | Set your social media profiles (Instagram, Snapchat, Facebook) to Private — only allow people you know to see your posts and personal information. | Public profiles are easy targets for cyberstalkers and identity thieves |
| 12 | Never post your college ID card, bank details, tickets, or government documents as photos on social media. | These contain sensitive information that can be misused |

---

### 🚨 Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you or anyone you know becomes a victim of cybercrime,
report it IMMEDIATELY through these official channels:

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| 🌐 National Cyber Crime Portal | cybercrime.gov.in — File an online complaint 24x7 |
| 📞 Cyber Crime Helpline | **1930** — Call anytime, available 24x7 across India |
| 🏛️ Local Police Station | File an FIR under IT Act 2000 for serious cybercrimes |
| 🏦 Your Bank | Call your bank immediately to freeze transactions if UPI fraud occurs |

> ⚠️ IMPORTANT: Report cybercrime as soon as possible.
> The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering
> lost money. Call **1930** immediately in case of financial fraud.

## 🔑 Quick Reference — Top 5 Rules to Never Forget

```
RULE 1: Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or password with ANYONE.
RULE 2: You NEVER need a PIN to RECEIVE money on UPI.
RULE 3: Always verify before you click, share, or pay.
RULE 4: Report cybercrime immediately at 1930 or cybercrime.gov.in
RULE 5: If it sounds too good to be true — it IS a scam.
```
