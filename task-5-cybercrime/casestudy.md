## 🔍 What is UPI / Online Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common
and rapidly growing cybercrimes in India. It involves criminals
tricking innocent people — especially students and young adults —
into sharing their UPI PINs, OTPs, or clicking on fake payment
links, resulting in unauthorized money being stolen directly from
their bank accounts.

## 📖 Case Study – A Realistic Scenario

**Title:** The Fake Scholarship Fraud – How a First-Year Student
Lost ₹15,000

**Background:**
Arjun, an 18-year-old first-year engineering student from Bhopal,
received a WhatsApp message from an unknown number claiming to be
from the "National Student Scholarship Board." The message stated
that Arjun had been selected for a ₹25,000 merit scholarship and
that he needed to complete a quick verification process to receive
the amount in his bank account.

**How the Crime Happened — Step by Step:**

Step 1 — Initial Contact:
The fraudster contacted Arjun via WhatsApp, presenting themselves
as an official government scholarship coordinator. The message
included a fake official-looking PDF with Arjun's name on it to
appear legitimate and build trust.

Step 2 — Building Trust:
The fraudster spoke confidently, used official-sounding language,
and even mentioned the name of Arjun's college to make the call
seem genuine. Arjun, excited about the scholarship, did not
question the authenticity of the caller.

Step 3 — The Trap:
The fraudster said that to "verify" Arjun's bank account and
transfer the scholarship amount, Arjun needed to accept a small
₹1 verification payment on his UPI app. The fraudster then sent
a UPI collect request — but it was actually a payment request
for ₹15,000, not a receipt.

Step 4 — The Theft:
Arjun, confused and excited, entered his UPI PIN thinking he was
receiving money. Instead, ₹15,000 was instantly debited from his
account. The fraudster immediately blocked Arjun on WhatsApp and
became unreachable.

Step 5 — Aftermath:
Arjun realized the fraud only after checking his bank balance.
He reported it to his family and the college, but recovering the
money was extremely difficult as the fraudster had used a fake
identity and a temporary SIM card.

**Who is Usually Targeted?**
- First-year college students who are new to UPI and digital payments
- Students from rural or semi-urban areas unfamiliar with online fraud
- Anyone who has recently applied for scholarships or job opportunities
- People who are financially stressed and are expecting money

**Consequences of the Crime:**
- Direct financial loss — ₹15,000 stolen in seconds
- Emotional distress, embarrassment, and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty recovering money as UPI transactions are instant and hard to reverse
- The fraudster faces legal action under the IT Act 2000 and IPC Section 420
  if caught, but many such criminals operate anonymously and are rarely caught
