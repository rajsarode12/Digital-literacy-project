## ✅ DO's — What You Should Do on Social Media

| No. | Do | Why It Matters |
|-----|----|----------------|
| 1 | ✅ Keep your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles updated regularly | Recruiters and companies check these profiles actively |
| 2 | ✅ Use a professional profile photo on LinkedIn | First impressions matter in professional networking |
| 3 | ✅ Share your academic achievements, projects, and certificates | Builds your credibility and professional brand online |
| 4 | ✅ Think before you post — ask "Would I show this to my professor?" | Prevents embarrassing or career-damaging posts |
| 5 | ✅ Use strong, unique passwords and enable 2FA on all accounts | Protects your accounts from hacking and identity theft |
| 6 | ✅ Follow and engage with professionals and companies in your field | Helps you stay updated with AI/tech industry trends |
| 7 | ✅ Report and block any account that harasses or bullies you | Protects your mental health and digital safety |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts — What You Should Never Do on Social Media

| No. | Don't | Why It Is Harmful |
|-----|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | ❌ Never share your password, OTP, or personal details online | Leads to account hacking and identity theft |
| 2 | ❌ Do not post offensive, hateful, or inappropriate content | Can result in disciplinary action or future job rejection |
| 3 | ❌ Never share fake news, unverified information, or rumors | Spreads misinformation and damages your reputation |
| 4 | ❌ Do not accept friend/follow requests from unknown strangers | Protects your privacy and prevents cyberstalking |
| 5 | ❌ Never post your location, daily routine, or home address publicly | Risks your physical safety and privacy |
| 6 | ❌ Do not engage in arguments or negativity in comment sections | Unprofessional behavior is visible to future employers |
| 7 | ❌ Never click on suspicious links sent via DMs or comments | May lead to phishing attacks or malware installation |
